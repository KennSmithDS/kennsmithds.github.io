Choropleth Map - % of survey respondents who always wear a mask

This is important because it shows which states and counties have either progressive populations, or maybe have stricter mandates. Mini Project Showcase

The visualizations belowe are generated using daily climatology data downloaded from NOAA's Climate Data Online Portal.

Dotplot for Seasonal Temperature and Precipitation - Okanogan and Chelan Counties

For this dotplot, the X-axis represents the daily precipitation and the Y-axis is the maximum temperature recorded, in a given daily observation. The data ranges from 2013 until beginning of October 2021, and includes features such as min and max temperature, precipitation and snow level on ground. There are more attributes available on NOAA's portal. This visualization is useful because it shows a linear relationship for each season between temperature and precipitation. The most obvious are a positive correlation between temperature and precipitation in fall, and a negative correlation between temperature and precipitation in summer. Such relationshisp are less clear in spring and winter.

Histogram of Max Temperatures by Season

For this histogram, we can confirm a few things that would be assumed to be true, e.g. that the hottest days would be in summer. However, this visualization also shows something counter intuitive, and that is that winter has a very similar distribution as spring, yet fall has the coldest days of the year. This can be observed by noticing the placement and height of stacked bars for the seasons.
